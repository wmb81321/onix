02 · Lockups

Stacked is primary. Horizontal is for tight spaces.

Use the stacked lockup for hero placements (signage, splash screens, app store). Use the horizontal lockup in headers, footers, and any place vertical space is constrained.

05 · Clear Space

Reserve a peer-radius of breathing room around the lockup.

Use the radius of one peer disc (x) as the minimum clear space on all sides. Do not let other elements, edges, or images cross into this zone.

Min. clear space = 1× peer radius (x)

06 · Do / Don't

Keep the geometry. Keep the palette. Keep the diamond solid.

✓ DOUSE THE FULL-COLOR LOCKUP
✗ DON'TRECOLOR THE PEERS
✗ DON'TOUTLINE THE DIAMOND — KEEP IT SOLID
✓ DOUSE MONO IN SINGLE-COLOR PRINT
✗ DON'TROTATE OR SKEW THE LOCKUP
✗ DON'TSTRETCH OR DISTORT

08 · Files & Figma Import

Drag any SVG into Figma. They come in as editable vector layers.

Open Figma, then drag the SVG file from Finder/Explorer onto the canvas (or use Place image). Each lockup arrives as a group of paths — peers, arcs, diamond, and wordmark — ready to recolor or recombine.

Figma import tips
  • Drag SVGs directly onto the Figma canvas — they import as editable vector groups.
  • Install Geist from Google Fonts before opening; otherwise Figma will substitute the wordmark font.
  • To re-style: select the peer circle, change fill — colors are not linked across files.
  • For brand variables: in Figma, create local color variables matching the hex codes in section 03.
